    SMTP

    SMTP stands for (Simple Mail Trnsfer Protocol). It is a communication oriented protocol by ASCII for electronic mail. i.e e-mail transmission

    • SMTP was first defined in 1982 by RFC 5821 to EXTENDED SMTP.
    • It uses process called store & process moving across the network.
    • Basic SMTP commands are :

    • HELLO:- Initiate a conversation with mail server.
    • MAIL:- Initiate who is sending the e-mail.
    • QUIT:- Indicates conversation is over.

    MIME

    This is the extended format of e-mail messages to support text in character-sets other than ASCII as-well-as attach audio, video, images,& OPP programs with the usage of RFC 822 format.
    MIME defines 5 new versions:
    • MIME-version.
    • content-description.
    • content-id.
    • content-transfer.
    • content-type.
